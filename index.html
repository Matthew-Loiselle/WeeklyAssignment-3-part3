Timeline


26 January 1947

Robert Cailliau is born in Tongeren, Belgium.


1969

Robert Cailliau graduates from Ghent UNiversity with a degree in electrical and mechanical engineering.


1987

Robert Cailliau is hired by CERN to work on the control systems of particle accelerators.


1987

Robert Cailliau is promoted to the leader of Office Computing Systems


1989

Tim Berners-Lee proposed an internet-based hypertext system, called the World Wide Web and Robert Cailliau co-authors a proposal to CERN to convince them to fund it


1991

Robert Cailliau organises the first public demonstration of the WWW at the Hypertext Conference in San Antonio.


1993

Robert Cailliau calls the first international WWW Conference, which was then held at CERN in 1994


1995

Robert Cailliau starts the "Wed for Schools", a program to introduce the web into schools as a resource.


2007

Robert Cailliau retires from CERN
